About "Edu-Hack Inspire"

EduHack Inspire is an initiative or event designed to bring together students, educators, and professionals to collaborate and innovate in the field of education through technology, creativity, and problem-solving. It typically involves a hackathon format, where participants work on creating solutions to challenges faced in the educational sector.

Terms and Condition

1. Participants must adhere to a code of conduct promoting inclusivity and respect. Discriminatory behavior will result in disqualification.
2. All project submissions must include a brief description, a demo video and source code. Specific guidelines for submission formats will be provided.
3. Use of prototyping and design tools is allowed, but all coding must be done during the hackathon hours. No pre-built solutions.
4. Each team will have a maximum of 5 minutes to present their project followed by a Q&A session. Timers will be strictly enforced
5. Limited technical support will be provided; teams are encouraged to troubleshoot issues independently.
6. Teams must check in with organizers at designated intervals to report on progress and receive any necessary support.
7. All projects must be finalized and submitted by the end of the 24-hour period. Late submissions will not be accepted.
8. In addition to innovation, functionality, design, and presentation, projects will also be evaluated based on user experience and scalability.
9. Any sensitive information shared by mentors or organizers during the event must be kept confidential and not disclosed to other participants.
10. Participants should prioritize their health; any signs of fatigue should be addressed with breaks or rest. Medical support is available on-site.
11. Participants are encouraged to provide constructive feedback to each other after presentations to foster community learning.
12. Any participant found violating the rules or engaging in unfair practices will be disqualified immediately, with no exceptions.
13. Maintain a proper Dress code for the hackathon
14. The submitted ideas will be evaluated on the following criteria: Innovation, Feasibility & Viability, Impact & Benefits, and Plagiarism & References.
15. Registration for the hackathon program is free for all participants.
16. First, we will shortlist your ideas and then selected teams should pay ₹200 per head.
17. The food (breakfast, lunch, dinner) will be provided for all the participants during hackathon program as well as snacks and refreshments in a regular interval.
18. The management is not responsible for your belongings & accessories, so please keep them safe (such as laptops, mobile phones, money and any kind of electric and non-electric belongings).
19. The team should consist of either only boys or only girls, as they participate separately.
 20. Form of misconduct, including disrespectful behavior, plagiarism, rule violations, or disruptive actions, will result in immediate disqualification and removal from the event.
